People

Silvia Ghirotto

Associate Professor

She is an associate professor in Genetics at the University of Ferrara. She is member of the center "World, Bones, Genes, Tools" at the University of Tübingen (Germany). Her research focuses in the reconstruction of evolutionary processes that shaped current and past patterns of genetic/genomic variation using both modern and ancient DNA data. She is particularly interested in exploring and interpreting human genetic variation together with evidence from other fields as anthropology, archaeology, linguistics. She is also primarily involved in the development and the analysis of statistical methods, mainly based on computer simulations, to infer past population dynamics and to test among different evolutionary scenarios.

Maria Teresa Vizzari

Postdoc

She studied prehistoric archaeology in Ferrara and received her PhD in Evolutionary Biology and Ecology from the University of Ferrara in 2021. Her research focuses mainly on the study of the genetic variability of Homo sapiens in its geographical and temporal dimensions, testing demographic hypotheses that combine genomic and archaeological data. She is also interested in the development of new frameworks that exploit both modern and ancient genomes, in order to better understand migration, kinship, and processes of cultural change during the evolution of our species.

Patrícia Santos

Postdoc

She studied Human Biology at the Faculty of Sciences of the University of Lisbon and received her PhD in Evolutionary Biology and Ecology from the University of Ferrara in 2020. Her research interests focus on how genomic data are influenced by the demographic history of populations and the methods used to improve our understanding of the human evolutionary history. Her current research activity is focused on the study of the Neolithic transition in Europe through the analysis of genomic and metagenomic data extracted from ancient human finds.

Rajiv Boscolo Agostini

PhD student

He received his master degree in Biomolecular and Evolutionary Sciences from the Univeristy of Ferrara in 2019. His research focuses mainly on demographic modeling and reconstruction of evolutionary dynamics. He is studying past human populations in Italy analyzing ancient whole-genome sequences using different approaches. He is also collaborating on projects involving different fields, such as employing bacterial markers for tracing human migrations or the reconstruction of demographic dynamics in invasive species.

Federico De Pizzol

Research fellow

He graduated in Evolutionary Biology at the University of Padua in 2022. His main interests are European medieval history and demography and skeletal anthropology. He is currently working on reconstructing the evolutionary dynamics of past human populations in Italy through the analysis of ancient genomes.
